# Lettering & Visual Style Notes

**Grim (the gun):** Grim's dialogue always uses a distinct balloon
style. Specifically, a jagged, angular balloon with an inverted or
tinted fill, tail pointing to the gun (or to the holster when
concealed). Only Atlas can hear Grim; no other character ever reacts to
his dialogue unless the script says otherwise.

**Precognition ("standard precog treatment"):** Atlas' precognition is
always drawn the same way: translucent ghost-images of the incoming
action, slightly offset from the present moment, rendered in a single
recurring accent colour. Panels reference this as the standard precog
treatment. The effect should read as foresight, not motion blur.

**Thought balloons vs. captions:** Thought balloons are Atlas' inner
voice within a scene; captions are reserved for time and place stamps.

# Scene 9 -- Entering the pipes

## Overview of the Pipes

The Pipes is a sprawling labyrinth located in the Undercity, and is a
relic of the city's industrial past. The vast network of pipes carried
water and steam to various parts of the city. As the city modernized and
moved upward into the Heights, the Pipes became irrelevant.

- A maze of rusty iron and steel, with pipes ranging from small ducts to
  large tunnels big enough to walk through. Some of the metal is
  corroded and pitted, rusty and tarnished.

- The air is thick with the smell of damp metal and old water

- Steam bursts through leaky valves and split seams, filling the area
  with a constant hiss that creates a haunting, rhythmic music (the
  music of the pipes).

- The Pipes aren't completely abandoned: the Mentor repurposed the area
  to serve as his hideout, and it includes a series of makeshift
  structures.

## Panel 1

**Description**: Wide shot of Atlas standing in front of the entrance to

**Thought:**

# Vignette: Bud

Bud. A deceptively evocative three-letter word that makes you think of a
loyal and loving dog or an old, reliable friend. Perhaps it was a
fitting name for an orphaned boy; pale-skinned, his body covered in dirt
and grime, hair wild and eyes filled with dreams. Bud spent his days
staring up at the distant lights of the Heights, hearing them call his
name from the only spot in the Seams free from the shadows cast by the
looming plateaus high above.

Growing up alone, without the comfort of a family or the warmth of even
a meager Undercity home was a seemingly impossible task for a kid, yet
it never bothered Bud. He seemed to always know which way the wind would
blow, and he made friends wherever he went. He was drawn to potential
like a moth to flame, and he had the uncanny ability to bring out the
best in others. He could see the things that others often overlooked.

He formed his first crew when he was twelve. He took the rejected 'Bigs'
and 'Smalls', those lacking the proper taste for violence the Seams
gangs eagerly desired and shared with them his contagious dreams of a
better life. One member of this motley crew, who bore a fresh tattoo of
a black pickaxe, marking him as a worker destined for the mines, would
later become Bud's best friend and brother.

The success of the crew was short-lived. More established gangs, seeing
the potential in those they once dismissed, began to poach members. And
Bud was okay with that. In his eyes, he turned the discarded into
accepted and that sat right with him. And as one door closed, another
opened: Bud found a family among his best friend Pyke's kin.

The mines became Bud's home, and despite never wearing the brand of a
miner, they counted him one among their kind. He spent the next several
years wandering the winding tunnels, adapting to a life underground, a
life largely devoid of the warmth of the sun.

As he grew, Bud retained his kindness, even as his best friend was drawn
into the dark allure of the Red Talon. They remained close as brothers,
despite their diverging paths. Bud never lost sight of his vision of a
better life, even in the depths of the mines.
